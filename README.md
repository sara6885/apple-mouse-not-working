# Magic Mouse Not Working? How Can I Fix My Magic Mouse

If your Apple Magic Mouse is not working, not connecting, or the cursor won’t move, you’re not alone. Many users face issues like the mouse being connected but not responding, lagging, or not tracking properly.

The good news: most **Magic Mouse not working** problems can be fixed quickly with a few simple steps.

---

## Signs Your Magic Mouse Is Not Working Properly

Before fixing the issue, identify what exactly is happening:

* Cursor not moving
* Mouse connected but not responding
* Scrolling not working
* Random lag or freezing
* Magic Mouse not connecting to Mac

These symptoms usually point to either hardware issues or connection problems.

---

## Why Your Apple Mouse Is Not Working

There are several common reasons why your **Apple mouse is not working**:

* Low battery or not charged
* Bluetooth connection issues
* Dirt blocking the sensor
* System glitches
* Interference from other devices

For example, even something simple like dust on the sensor can stop tracking completely. 

---

## Fix #1: Check Battery or Charge the Mouse

One of the most common reasons:

* Make sure your Magic Mouse is charged
* If you use an older model, replace the batteries
* Check that the green light is visible

Low power can cause lag, disconnects, or no response at all. 

---

## Fix #2: Turn Magic Mouse Off and On

This simple step often fixes connection issues.

### Steps:

1. Flip the mouse over
2. Turn it OFF
3. Wait 5–10 seconds
4. Turn it back ON

Restarting the device can reset temporary glitches.

---

## Fix #3: Restart Your Mac

If your **Magic Mouse is not connecting to Mac**, try:

* Restart your Mac
* Turn Bluetooth off and on again

## Fix #4: Reconnect Magic Mouse via Bluetooth

Sometimes your mouse needs to be paired again.

### Steps:

1. Go to **System Settings → Bluetooth**

<img width="1400" height="866" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/dc392a27-7014-4ae1-a7bb-5ca6ee550b44" />

2. Remove your Magic Mouse
3. Turn the mouse off and on
4. Click **Connect**

Re-pairing fixes most **Magic Mouse not connecting** problems. 

---

## Fix #5: Clean the Sensor (Very Important)

If the cursor doesn’t move:

* Turn the mouse upside down
* Check the sensor area
* Blow air or gently clean it

Even small dust particles can block tracking and stop the mouse from working. 

---

## Fix #6: Check for Bluetooth Interference

Wireless interference can break the connection.

### Try this:

* Move closer to your Mac
* Turn off other Bluetooth devices
* Avoid crowded wireless environments

---

## Fix #7: Reset Bluetooth Module (Advanced)

If your **Magic Mouse still not working**:

### Steps:

1. Hold **Shift + Option**
2. Click Bluetooth icon
3. Select **Reset Bluetooth module**

4. Restart your Mac

This fixes deeper connection issues.

---

## Fix #8: Make Sure the Mouse Is Turned On

It sounds obvious, but:

* Check the switch at the bottom
* Ensure the green light is visible

Sometimes the mouse may accidentally be turned off. 

---

## Fix #9: Use Cable Pairing (If Available)

You can connect your Magic Mouse to your Mac with a cable temporarily:

* Plug it in
* Wait a moment
* Disconnect

This can force pairing and fix connection problems. 

---

## Fix #10: Check for Hardware Problems

If nothing works:

* The sensor may be damaged
* Internal components may be faulty
* Battery issues may exist

In this case, repair or replacement may be needed.

---

## Why Magic Mouse Cursor Does Not Move

If your cursor is stuck:

* The sensor might be dirty
* The surface might not be suitable
* The mouse may not be tracking correctly

Cleaning the sensor is often the fastest fix.

---

## Final Thoughts

If your **Magic Mouse is not working**, don’t worry—most issues are simple:

* Charge the mouse
* Restart devices
* Reconnect via Bluetooth
* Clean the sensor

In most cases, you can fix the problem in just a few minutes without replacing your mouse.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why is my Magic Mouse connected but not working?

It could be due to low battery, Bluetooth issues, or a dirty sensor.

---

### Why is my Magic Mouse not moving the cursor?

Most likely caused by dirt on the sensor or surface issues.

---

### How do I fix Magic Mouse not connecting to Mac?

Restart your Mac, turn Bluetooth off/on, and reconnect the mouse.

---

### Can a dirty sensor stop Magic Mouse from working?

Yes. Even small dust particles can block tracking completely.

---

### What should I do if nothing works?

Try resetting Bluetooth or consider hardware repair.
